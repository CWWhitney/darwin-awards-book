We Did Not Win a Darwin Award

Chapter 5 Wildlife Encounters

“Between a human and a bear, the bear is not the one making poor decisions.” - Park Ranger wisdom

5.1 Introduction

Wild animals are wild for a reason. They are unpredictable, powerful, and operating on instincts honed over millions of years of evolution. Humans, on the other hand, often operate on a dangerous combination of curiosity, overconfidence, and a fundamental misunderstanding of the phrase “apex predator.”

This chapter collects stories from people who forgot that animals have teeth, claws, venom, and absolutely no obligation to tolerate human stupidity.

5.13 Lessons from the Animal Kingdom

What these stories teach us:

  1. Wild animals are wild - No matter how cute, how small, or how habituated to humans
  2. Teeth and claws evolved for a reason - Usually that reason is defense or predation
  3. You are not Steve Irwin - And even he had limitations
  4. Personal space applies to animals - Give them distance
  5. Food brings danger - Never feed wild animals
  6. Mothers are protective - Never get between a mother and her young
  7. Mating season is dangerous - Animals are aggressive when hormonal
  8. Fast beats slow - Most animals can outrun you
  9. Strong beats weak - Most animals are stronger than you think
  10. Respect saves lives - Yours and theirs

5.14 Statistical Reality Check

According to various wildlife agencies:

  • Hippos kill ~500 people per year (most dangerous large land mammal)
  • Elephants kill ~500 people per year
  • Crocodiles kill ~1,000 people per year
  • Snakes kill ~100,000 people per year
  • Dogs kill ~25,000 people per year (mostly through rabies)
  • Mosquitoes kill ~700,000 people per year (through disease)

For comparison: - Sharks kill ~10 people per year - Bears kill ~1-5 people per year in North America - Wolves kill <1 person per year

The animals we fear are often not the ones we should fear. But we should respect all of them.
